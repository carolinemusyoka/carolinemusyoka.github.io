The waterfront terrace at sunset is something straight out of a film — dhows crossing the channel with their sails up, the call to prayer drifting across the water, and a gin and tonic sweating in your hand. The rooms are simple but elegant in the way that only old-money coastal hotels manage. White linen, dark wood, ceiling fans turning slowly. No TV, no minibar buzzing. Just the sound of the ocean and the wind in the palms. The seafood here is exceptional. Fresh from the morning catch, prepared simply, served on the terrace. The staff remember your name by day two and your drink order by day three. Old-world charm that hasn't been ruined by renovation. If you need to slow down, this is where you come.
