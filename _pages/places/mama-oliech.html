--- layout: place title: "Mama Oliech" permalink: /places/mama-oliech/ place_category: eat location: "Nairobi, Kenya" rating: 5 price: "" image: "https://ik.imagekit.io/ngmub3qdz/tr:w-1400,f-auto,q-80/cities/nairobi/mama-oliech.jpg" info: - icon: "utensils" label: "Cuisine" value: "Traditional Kenyan — Luo specialities" - icon: "tag" label: "Price range" value: "KES 500–800 per meal" - icon: "star" label: "Must try" value: "Fried tilapia, ugali, kachumbari" - icon: "clock" label: "Hours" value: "10am–9pm, cash only" --- Every Nairobian has an opinion about Mama Oliech. Debates about whether it's the best or merely excellent have started arguments that last decades. But most people agree on one thing: the fried tilapia here is as good as it gets in this city. The fish is fresh from the lake. Deep-fried to a crispy golden perfection that crunches when you break into it, revealing flesh that's still moist and flaky. Served with a mountain of ugali (dense, satisfying, the way it should be) and sukuma wiki that's been prepared with actual care. No frills. No pretension. Plastic tables, a queue at lunch that's long but moves fast, and food that makes you close your eyes with the first bite. Cash only — there's an ATM nearby. This is Nairobi eating at its most honest. Come hungry, leave happy.
