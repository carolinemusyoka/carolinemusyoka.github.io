--- layout: place title: "Wildebeest Eco Camp" permalink: /places/wildebeest-eco-camp/ place_category: stay location: "Nairobi, Kenya" rating: 3 price: "$$" image: "https://ik.imagekit.io/ngmub3qdz/tr:w-1400,f-auto,q-80/cities/nairobi.jpg" info: - icon: "map" label: "Location" value: "Quiet Nairobi suburb" - icon: "tag" label: "Price range" value: "KES 2,000–8,000 per night" - icon: "users" label: "Best for" value: "Solo travellers, backpackers" - icon: "wifi" label: "Amenities" value: "Garden bar, fire pit, Wi-Fi, breakfast" --- Somewhere between backpackers' hostel and boutique lodge, and it works. The garden bar is the beating heart — fairy lights strung between trees, a fire pit that gets lit every evening, and travellers from every continent sharing stories over Tuskers. Rooms range from dorms to private cottages. The cottages are worth the upgrade — small but clean, with actual doors and hot water that's reliably hot. The dorms are standard but well-maintained. It's in a quiet Nairobi suburb so you need a taxi to get anywhere, but that's part of the appeal — you're away from the noise. The breakfast buffet is surprisingly solid: eggs made to order, fresh fruit, good coffee. The Wi-Fi actually works, which in Nairobi backpackers is worth at least an extra star.
