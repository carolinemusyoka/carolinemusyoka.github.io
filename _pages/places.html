Diani, Kenya

Boxo Diani

★★★★☆ $$$

The vibe here is unmatched on the south coast. Container-chic rooms that are actually comfortable, a pool that catches the sun all afternoon, and a DJ most evenings who reads the room perfectly. It's lively without being obnoxious. The beach is right there — literally walk thirty seconds and you're in the Indian Ocean. Staff are friendly and the check-in is refreshingly hassle-free. If you're under 40 and want energy with your beach trip, this is the spot.

Best for: groups, couples who like energy Book via: Boxo Diani website or Booking.com

Diani, Kenya

Lala Galu

★★★★☆ $$$

Sister property to Boxo, same management, same stretch of beach — but quieter. The rooms are more traditional, the pace is slower, and it attracts a slightly older crowd. Perfect if you want the Diani beach lifestyle but don't need the poolside DJ. The restaurant is shared with Boxo so you get the best of both. Walking between the two takes about two minutes.

Best for: couples, families, quiet stays Book via: Lala Galu website

Stone Town, Zanzibar

Emerson on Hurumzi

★★★★★ $$$$

This place is an experience. A restored merchant's house in the heart of Stone Town, every room filled with Zanzibari antiques — carved beds, Persian rugs, stained glass. The rooftop restaurant serves a set Swahili menu at sunset and it's one of the best meals you'll eat in East Africa. Not cheap, but genuinely unforgettable. The kind of hotel that ruins all other hotels for you.

Best for: honeymoons, special occasions, history lovers Book via: Emerson Zanzibar website

Lamu, Kenya

Peponi Hotel

★★★★★ $$$$

Peponi has been the address in Lamu for decades and it still earns it. The waterfront terrace at sunset is something out of a film — dhows crossing the channel, the call to prayer drifting across the water. Rooms are simple but elegant, the seafood is exceptional, and the staff remember your name by day two. Old-world charm that hasn't been ruined by renovation.

Best for: writers, romantics, anyone who needs to slow down Book via: Peponi Hotel website
Nairobi, Kenya

Wildebeest Eco Camp

★★★☆☆ $$

Somewhere between backpackers' hostel and boutique lodge. The garden bar is genuinely great — fairy lights, fire pit, travellers from everywhere. Rooms range from dorms to private cottages. It's in a quiet Nairobi suburb so you need a taxi to get anywhere, but that's part of the charm. The breakfast buffet is surprisingly solid and the Wi-Fi actually works.

Best for: solo travellers, backpackers, budget-conscious Book via: Hostelworld or walk-in
Kanunga, Kenya

Kanunga Lakeside Resort

★★★☆☆ $$

A small, no-frills resort near Ndakaini Dam that punches above its weight. The gardens are well-kept, the rooms are clean, and the view over the dam makes up for anything else. It's the kind of place weekend day-trippers from Nairobi come for lunch, but staying overnight means you get the misty morning views that everyone else misses. Don't expect luxury — expect peace.

Kanunga, Kenya

Ndakaini Dam

★★★★☆

You wouldn't expect Nairobi's main water supply to be this beautiful. The dam sits quietly in the Gatundu highlands, surrounded by tea estates and patches of forest. The water is a deep teal that changes shade through the day. There's no tourist infrastructure at all — no entrance fee, no signs, no crowd. Just you, the water, and the hills. Bring a packed lunch and stay awhile.

Getting there: 1.5hrs from Nairobi via Thika Entry: Free

Taveta, Kenya

Lake Chala

★★★★★

A volcanic crater lake straddling the Kenya-Tanzania border. The water is emerald green and impossibly clear. You descend steep crater walls through dense bush to reach it — the walk down takes about 20 minutes and the reward is a lake that feels completely untouched. You can kayak, swim, or just sit on the rocks and stare. One of the most beautiful freshwater spots in East Africa.

Getting there: 6hrs from Nairobi, near Taveta Entry: KES 500

Aberdare NP, Kenya

Karuru Falls

★★★★★

At 273 metres, Karuru is one of Kenya's tallest waterfalls and one of its least visited. Three cascading tiers dropping through highland forest into a mist-filled gorge. The drive through the Aberdares to get here is half the experience — bamboo groves, giant lobelias, and colobus monkeys. You'll need a 4x4 and KWS park fees but it's worth every shilling.

Getting there: 3hrs from Nairobi via Nyeri Entry: KWS park fees (KES 1,000 for citizens)

Rift Valley, Kenya

The Escarpment Viewpoint

★★★★★

The signed viewpoint on the Nairobi-Naivasha road where the Great Rift Valley suddenly opens below you. It's a 600-metre drop and on clear days you can see Lake Naivasha, Mount Longonot, and the far western wall of the rift. I've stopped here dozens of times and it never gets old. Best at sunrise or late afternoon when the light makes the valley glow.

Getting there: 45 mins from Nairobi Entry: Free (roadside viewpoint)

Wasini, Kenya

Wasini Island

★★★★★

The day trip that made the whole Diani trip. Dolphins in the morning, a seafood feast cooked by the local women's cooperative, then swimming on a sandbar that only appears at low tide. It sounds like a tourist trap but it's run by the community and feels authentic. Book at Shimoni pier, KES 4,500 all-inclusive. Go early and bring reef-safe sunscreen.

Getting there: 1hr south of Diani + boat Cost: KES 4,500 all-inclusive

Kanunga, Kenya

Kanunga Waterfall

★★★★☆

A short but slippery hike from the road through farmland and forest to a powerful waterfall that throws mist twenty metres into the air. It's not the tallest falls in Kenya but the setting is beautiful — moss-covered rocks, forest canopy, and total solitude. Bring waterproof gear for your camera and wear shoes with grip. Best after the rains when the flow is at its strongest.

Getting there: Near Ndakaini Dam Entry: Free

Stone Town, Zanzibar

Forodhani Night Market

★★★★★

Every evening the waterfront transforms into an open-air food market. Smoke rising from dozens of grills, vendors calling out, the smell of charcoal and spices hanging in the salt air. Zanzibar pizza is the star — a thin dough parcel stuffed with egg, meat, and vegetables, cooked on a flat griddle. Walk the entire row first, then commit. Everything is fresh, everything is cheap, and the ocean is right there.

Must try: Zanzibar pizza, mishkaki, sugar cane juice Price: TZS 3,000–8,000 per plate

Nairobi, Kenya

Mama Oliech

★★★★★

Every Nairobian has an opinion about Mama Oliech and most of them are right — it's the best fried tilapia in the city. The fish is fresh from the lake, deep-fried to crispy perfection, served with ugali and sukuma wiki. No frills, no pretension, just food that makes you close your eyes. Cash only. Queue at lunch is long but moves fast. Go hungry.

Must try: fried tilapia, ugali, kachumbari Price: KES 500–800 per meal
Diani, Kenya

Ali Barbour's Cave Restaurant

★★★★★

A restaurant inside a natural coral cave. That's not a metaphor — you descend steps into an actual cave with the sky visible through the open roof above. Candles on every surface, white linen on the tables, and a seafood menu that takes the location seriously. It's romantic bordering on theatrical and completely worth it. Book ahead because there are maybe fifteen tables total.

Must try: grilled lobster, coconut fish curry Price: KES 3,000–6,000 per person

Lamu, Kenya

Bush Gardens

★★★★☆

A courtyard restaurant hidden in the backstreets of Lamu Old Town. The prawn curry is legendary — rich, coconut-heavy, with a slow chilli burn that builds. Fish is grilled whole over charcoal and served with chapati and coconut beans. Nothing on the menu costs much and the portions are generous. Get the passion fruit juice and sit in the garden under the bougainvillea.

Must try: prawn curry, grilled fish, passion fruit juice Price: KES 400–1,000 per meal

Diani, Kenya

Diani Beach

★★★★★

White sand that squeaks underfoot, water so turquoise it looks edited, and a coastline that stretches for kilometres without getting crowded. The morning is best — low tide exposes tidal pools and the light is soft and golden. Afternoon brings the kite surfers and the beach bars fill up. Walk south from Boxo for the emptier stretches. Bring reef shoes for the coral.

Best time: early morning or sunset Vibe: beach walks, swimming, kite watching

Nairobi, Kenya

Karura Forest

★★★★★

A thousand hectares of indigenous forest in the middle of one of Africa's biggest cities. It shouldn't exist but it does, and it's incredible. Walking trails, running paths, a waterfall, caves, and the kind of silence that makes you forget the Thika Superhighway is two kilometres away. Entry is cheap, the paths are well-marked, and you can easily spend three hours here without covering it all.

Best time: weekend mornings, weekday afternoons Entry: KES 100 (citizens), KES 600 (visitors)
Nairobi, Kenya

Alchemist Bar

★★★★☆

Nairobi's creative scene distilled into one shipping container complex. Art on the walls, DJ booths in converted containers, craft cocktails, street food stalls, and a crowd that ranges from photographers to tech founders to fashion designers. It's the kind of place that starts slow on a Friday and doesn't stop until the sun comes up. Weekends are best. Dress however you want.

Best time: Friday and Saturday nights Vibe: art, cocktails, live music, street food
Stone Town, Zanzibar

Forodhani Waterfront

★★★★★

Find a spot on the seawall, buy a sugar cane juice from a vendor, and watch the sun set behind dhow silhouettes on the Indian Ocean. That's it. That's the whole activity. And it's one of the best hours you'll spend in East Africa. The light goes from gold to pink to deep orange and the whole town seems to pause. Free, effortless, perfect.

Best time: 5:30 PM onwards Vibe: sunset, people-watching, zero agenda

Lamu, Kenya

Shela Beach

★★★★★

Twelve kilometres of sand and almost nobody on it. Walk from Lamu Old Town along the waterfront to Shela village (45 minutes), then keep going. The beach stretches south into dunes that look like they belong in the Sahara. No sun loungers, no hawkers, no beach bars. Just sand, sea, and the occasional fisherman. Bring water and a hat because there's no shade.

Best time: morning walk from Lamu Town Vibe: solitude, photography, long walks
Diani, Kenya

Nomad Beach Bar

★★★★☆

Right on the sand, lanterns strung between palm trees, grilled calamari and cold beers. The kind of place where you sit down for dinner and end up staying until the bar closes. The food is reliably good — not fine dining, just well-executed beach food. The evening vibe is perfect: relaxed, warm, slightly buzzy. Toes in the sand, stars overhead. What more do you need.

Best time: sunset drinks into dinner Vibe: beach bar, seafood, candlelit
