The Nairobi–Naivasha drive is one of those routes that locals take for granted but visitors never forget. In under two hours you go from the chaotic energy of the capital to the wide-open floor of the Great Rift Valley, dropping nearly 500 metres in elevation along the way.

It's not a long drive, but it packs in more dramatic scenery per kilometre than almost any other road in Kenya. Tea plantations, eucalyptus forests, the escarpment hairpins, and then — suddenly — the valley.

I've done this drive dozens of times and it never gets old.
