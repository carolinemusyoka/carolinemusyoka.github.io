I went to the mountains to think. Not to hike or conquer a summit — just to sit somewhere high and quiet and let the noise drain away.

The air is different up there. Thinner, cleaner, almost sharp. Sounds carry further. You can hear a river from a ridge away, birdsong from across a valley. And in between those sounds, silence — real silence, the kind that cities never have.

## Above the Cloud Line

There's a moment in the early morning, before the sun fully rises, when the valleys fill with cloud and you're standing above it. The world below disappears and you're left with just sky and rock and the feeling that you've stepped outside of time.

## What the Mountains Teach

Mountains don't care about your deadlines or your plans. They've been here for millions of years and they'll be here long after we're gone. There's something deeply calming about that perspective.

I came down from the mountains with fewer photographs than I expected but a much clearer head. Sometimes the best thing a trip can give you isn't content for your camera roll — it's space to breathe.
