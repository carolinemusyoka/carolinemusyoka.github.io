Stone Town is a maze. Narrow alleyways twist between coral-stone buildings, past carved wooden doors and through archways that open into unexpected courtyards. Every corner has a story, and every door has a history.

I wandered for hours without a map, which is really the only way to experience this place. You turn a corner and find a group of kids playing football in a courtyard. Another turn and you're at the waterfront, watching dhows drift across the harbour at sunset.

## The Doors

The famous carved doors of Stone Town are more than decoration — they're a record of the island's history. The patterns tell you about the family who lived there, their trade, their origins. Brass studs, intricate carvings, layers of paint from different eras.

## Night Markets

As the sun drops, Forodhani Gardens comes alive. Smoke rises from dozens of grills, the smell of fresh seafood fills the air, and the whole town seems to gather in one place. It's chaotic, loud, and absolutely wonderful.

Stone Town reminds you that some places resist being understood quickly. You have to earn their stories by getting lost in them.
