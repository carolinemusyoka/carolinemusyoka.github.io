Nairobi Naivasha

Nairobi to Naivasha

March 10, 2026 Rift Valley, Kenya
91 km Total Distance
1.5 hrs Drive Time
Easy Difficulty
Year-round Best Season

The Nairobi–Naivasha drive is one of those routes that locals take for granted but visitors never forget. In under two hours you go from the chaotic energy of the capital to the wide-open floor of the Great Rift Valley, dropping nearly 500 metres in elevation along the way.

It’s not a long drive, but it packs in more dramatic scenery per kilometre than almost any other road in Kenya. Tea plantations, eucalyptus forests, the escarpment hairpins, and then — suddenly — the valley.

I’ve done this drive dozens of times and it never gets old.

Along the Way

Scenic Viewpoints

Limuru Tea Estates Overlook

Just past Limuru town, the road opens up to rolling green tea plantations that stretch to the horizon. On a clear morning the view extends all the way to the Aberdares. Pull over at the layby on the left — it's worth the five minutes.

KM 28 ↑ 2100m

The Great Rift Escarpment Viewpoint

This is the moment the drive becomes unforgettable. The road drops off the escarpment and the entire Rift Valley opens below you — wide, hazy, impossibly vast. There's a signed viewpoint with parking on the left side.

KM 52 ↑ 2300m

Lake Naivasha Approach

As you descend to the valley floor, the landscape shifts from highland green to golden savannah. Watch for zebra and giraffe along the last 10km stretch as the lake comes into view.

KM 85 ↑ 1884m

Pit Stops

Where to Stop

Fuel

Shell Station — Limuru

Last reliable fuel stop before the descent. Clean washrooms, mini-mart, and a decent coffee shop. Fill up here.

Food

Kentmere Club

Colonial-era country club with a restaurant serving solid breakfasts and lunch. Beautiful gardens, very peaceful. A proper sit-down stop.

Food

Mai Mahiu Roadside Grills

At the base of the escarpment, smoky roadside stalls serve the best nyama choma (grilled meat) you'll find on this route. Cheap, filling, and full of character.

Parking

Hell's Gate National Park Entrance

If you want to break the drive with a hike or cycle through the gorge, Hell's Gate is just south of Naivasha. Secure parking, KWS facilities, and bike rentals at the gate.

Before You Go

Driving Tips

Leave Nairobi before 7 AM to avoid Waiyaki Way traffic — it can add an hour to your drive.

The escarpment descent has sharp hairpin turns. Drop to second gear and take it slow, especially when it's wet.

Fuel up at Limuru. There are stations in Mai Mahiu but Limuru's is more reliable.

Best light for photography is early morning — the escarpment viewpoint faces east and catches beautiful golden hour.

Bring a light jacket. Limuru sits at 2,100m and it's noticeably cooler than Nairobi.

If you're continuing to Nakuru, the road from Naivasha is flat and fast. Budget another hour.
